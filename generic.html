representation of mastication in the primate orofacial sensorimotor cortex

ARCE-MCSHANE LABORATORY

Goal

Complex orofacial sensorimotor behaviors, such as feeding and speech, rely on precise control from the orofacial sensorimotor cortex (oSM)1. How chewing on either left or right side of different food types is represented by populations of neurons in the primary motor cortex (M1) and primary somatosensory cortex (S1) is still poorly understood. Here, we evaluated the cortical representation of chew-sides and food-types in oSM and how this was affected by the absence of tactile inputs to the oral cavity.

Tool

Demixed principal-components-analysis(dPCA) could be implemented to decompose the dependencies of the population activity into a task-independent parameter of time (for activity related to the progression through the behavioral trial), and task-dependent parameters of chew-side and food-type, and the interaction between them.

Kinematics: The novel use of bilateral videoradiography with the X-Ray Reconstruction of Moving Morphology (XROMM) workflow allows us to track radio-opaque markers implanted in the tongue and mandible at a high spatio-temporal resolution. Thus, we are able to reconstruct the markers’ 3D position and determine the chewing side.
Electrophysiology: Two 96-channel Utah arrays were chronically implanted; one in M1 and another in S1.
Behavior: We categorized gape cycles (± 0.75 second relative to minimum gape) into left or right chews of either grape or gummy bear (Fig. 2).
